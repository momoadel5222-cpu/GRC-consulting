Phase 1: Customer Identification

Collect and verify customer information

1
Information Collection
Gather required customer data and documentation
  • Full legal name and any aliases
  • Date and place of birth
  • Residential address
  • Contact information
  • Nationality and citizenship
  • Occupation and employer
2
Document Verification
Verify identity using official documents
  • Government-issued ID (passport, driver's license)
  • Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement)
  • For businesses: incorporation documents
  • Tax identification number
  • Verify document authenticity
3
Beneficial Ownership
Identify ultimate beneficial owners (25%+ ownership)
  • Identify individuals with significant control
  • Ownership structure documentation
  • Ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs)
  • Verify UBO identities

Phase 2: Risk Assessment

Evaluate customer risk profile

4
Customer Risk Rating
Assign risk level based on multiple factors
  • Geographic risk (country/region)
  • Customer type (individual, corporate, PEP)
  • Product/service risk
  • Delivery channel risk
  • Transaction patterns expected
5
Screening Checks
Screen against regulatory lists and databases
  • Sanctions lists (OFAC, UN, EU)
  • PEP databases
  • Adverse media screening
  • Law enforcement lists
  • Internal watchlists
6
Source of Funds/Wealth
Understand the origin of customer's assets
  • Employment income verification
  • Business revenue sources
  • Inheritance or gifts documentation
  • Investment returns
  • Asset liquidation records

Phase 3: Approval & Monitoring

Final approval and ongoing surveillance

7
Account Approval
Obtain necessary approvals based on risk level
  • Low risk: Automated or first-line approval
  • Medium risk: Compliance officer approval
  • High risk: Senior management approval
  • Document approval rationale
8
Account Setup
Create customer profile and set monitoring parameters
  • Set transaction limits
  • Configure monitoring rules
  • Assign risk rating in system
  • Schedule periodic review dates
9
Ongoing Monitoring
Continuous surveillance and periodic reviews
  • Transaction monitoring (real-time)
  • Periodic KYC refresh (annually/bi-annually)
  • Trigger event reviews (unusual activity)
  • Re-screening against updated lists
