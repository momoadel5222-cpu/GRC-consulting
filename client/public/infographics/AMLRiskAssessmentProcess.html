Comprehensive Framework for Money Laundering Risk Evaluation

1
Risk Identification
Identify and document all potential ML/TF risks across the organization
  • Customer risk factors (PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions)
  • Product and service risks (cash-intensive, cross-border)
  • Geographic risks (sanctions, corruption levels)
  • Delivery channel risks (non-face-to-face, agents)
2
Risk Analysis
Evaluate the likelihood and potential impact of identified risks
  • Assess inherent risk (before controls)
  • Analyze historical data and trends
  • Consider regulatory guidance and typologies
  • Review industry best practices
3
Risk Evaluation
Rate and prioritize risks using a consistent scoring methodology
  • Apply risk rating methodology (Low, Medium, High, Critical)
  • Calculate composite risk scores
  • Prioritize risks requiring immediate attention
  • Document risk appetite and tolerance levels
4
Control Assessment
Evaluate existing controls and their effectiveness
  • Review policies, procedures, and systems
  • Test control effectiveness
  • Identify control gaps and weaknesses
  • Calculate residual risk (after controls)
5
Risk Mitigation
Develop and implement action plans to address identified risks
  • Design enhanced due diligence measures
  • Strengthen transaction monitoring rules
  • Implement additional controls where needed
  • Assign responsibilities and timelines
6
Monitoring & Review
Continuously monitor risks and update assessment regularly
  • Conduct periodic risk assessment reviews (annually minimum)
  • Monitor changes in risk profile
  • Update assessment for new products/markets
  • Report findings to senior management and board
Risk Rating Categories

LOW RISK

Simplified due diligence may apply

MEDIUM RISK

Standard CDD procedures

HIGH RISK

Enhanced due diligence required

CRITICAL RISK

Senior approval & ongoing monitoring

Risk-Based Approach | Aligned with FATF Recommendations

Assessment should be documented, reviewed regularly, and approved by senior management
